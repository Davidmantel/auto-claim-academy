🚐💧 Why Water Damage Claims on RVs Get Denied

1. Neglected Maintenance
  • • Deteriorated seals, cracked caulking, or worn roof membranes
  • • Leaks from aging plumbing or unmaintained water lines
  • • Insurers expect regular upkeep — damage from neglect is excluded
2. Gradual Damage
  • • Slow leaks over time (e.g. roof seepage, condensation buildup)
  • • Mold or mildew from long-term moisture exposure
  • • Only sudden and accidental damage is covered — gradual issues are not
3. Manufacturer Defects
  • • Poor installation of roof, windows, or plumbing
  • • Faulty seals or design flaws
  • • These are usually excluded unless covered by a separate warranty
4. Improper Winterization
  • • Burst pipes due to freezing temperatures
  • • Damage from not draining water systems or adding antifreeze
  • • Considered preventable — often denied
5. Excluded Perils
  • • Flooding, earthquakes, or sewer backup may not be covered unless added as endorsements
  • • Standard policies may not include natural disasters
